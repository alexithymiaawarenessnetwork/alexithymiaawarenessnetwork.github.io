R. Michael Bagby

A concise, source-backed research-context note on R. Michael Bagby's role in AAN's alexithymia assessment source trail.

This is a brief research-context page, not a full biography. It includes only claims AAN can support from citations and public-profile evidence.

Why Bagby appears on AAN

R. Michael Bagby is included here because his name appears on several recurring AAN source trails for alexithymia assessment and affect-regulation research:

  • the 1994 TAS-20 item-selection and cross-validation paper, co-authored with James D. A. Parker and Graeme J. Taylor (Bagby, Parker, and Taylor, 1994);
  • the 1997 book Disorders of Affect Regulation: Alexithymia in Medical and Psychiatric Illness, co-authored with Graeme J. Taylor and James D. A. Parker (Taylor, Bagby, and Parker, 1997);
  • the 2006 Toronto Structured Interview for Alexithymia paper, co-authored with Taylor, Parker, and Susan E. Dickens (Bagby, Taylor, Parker, and Dickens, 2006);
  • a 2020 review marking twenty-five years of the TAS-20 literature, co-authored with Parker and Taylor (Bagby, Parker, and Taylor, 2020).

For AAN readers, the reason to include Bagby is bibliographic and historical: his published work is part of the literature around the Toronto Alexithymia Scale-20 (TAS-20), the Toronto Structured Interview for Alexithymia (TSIA), and affect-regulation framing in alexithymia research.

TAS-20 and TSIA context

The TAS-20 is a self-report measurement instrument used in alexithymia research. AAN describes it as a screening or research measure, not as a diagnosis by itself. Scores should be interpreted carefully, especially outside research settings or without professional context.

The TSIA is an interview-based assessment instrument described in the 2006 development paper. Mentioning the TSIA here does not mean AAN offers assessment, training, scoring guidance, or clinical interpretation.
