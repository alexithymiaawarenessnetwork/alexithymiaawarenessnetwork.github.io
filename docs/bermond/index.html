Emotion researcher profile

Bob Bermond

BVAQ co-author and assessment context.

Bob Bermond is listed, with Harrie C. M. Vorst, as an author of the 2001 validation paper for the Bermond–Vorst Alexithymia Questionnaire (BVAQ), a self-report alexithymia measure used in research (Vorst and Bermond, 2001).

BVAQ relevance

The BVAQ is an assessment instrument, not a diagnosis. It is one research measure among several alexithymia instruments, and its validation paper is part of the literature behind alexithymia measurement.

Use BVAQ claims cautiously:

  • It is a self-report questionnaire used in alexithymia research.
  • It is associated with cognitive and affective dimensions in the BVAQ literature.
  • It should not be used by itself to diagnose a person or replace professional assessment.
  • Detailed scoring, cutoffs, translations, subtype claims, and clinical-use claims should come from qualified sources in an appropriate setting.

Name and biography note

The BVAQ validation paper lists the name form “Bob Bermond.” This page does not establish a current institutional profile, full biography, or broader professional-role claim.
