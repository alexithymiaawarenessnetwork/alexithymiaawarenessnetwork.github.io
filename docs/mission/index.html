Mission

A concise statement of what the Alexithymia Awareness Network is here to do.

The Alexithymia Awareness Network (AAN) is an informational, community-oriented project about alexithymia. Our work is meant to help people find plain-language explanations, credible research, and supportive next steps without treating a website as a clinician.

What AAN is here to do

  • Increase awareness of alexithymia in clear, non-stigmatizing language.
  • Make information easier to find by connecting readers with cited explainers, research papers, support resources, and practical learning paths.
  • Respect lived experience while keeping public claims modest, accurate, and separate from personal medical advice.
  • Support further research and learning by preserving references, identifying evidence gaps, and improving how alexithymia resources are organized.
  • Build community carefully by making it easier for people with alexithymia, families, clinicians, researchers, and allies to orient themselves and ask better questions.

What AAN does not do

AAN does not diagnose alexithymia, provide therapy, prescribe treatment, or replace qualified care. Screening tools and educational pages can be useful starting points, but personal questions about mental health, assessment, or treatment belong with qualified professionals.

For first steps, see the FAQ, Resources, Research Papers, or Support.
