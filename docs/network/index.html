Who AAN is for

A careful overview of who AAN serves, what the network means, and what it does not provide.

The Alexithymia Awareness Network is an informational and community-oriented project for people trying to understand alexithymia, and for people who support, study, teach, or work with them.

AAN is not a clinic, emergency service, formal membership association, or professional referral network. The site offers educational orientation and contact pathways, not diagnosis, treatment, or personalized clinical advice.

Who may find AAN useful

AAN is intended for several overlapping audiences:

  • people who relate to alexithymia or are wondering whether the concept fits their experience;
  • family members, partners, friends, and supporters who want clearer language for conversations;
  • clinicians, educators, coaches, and peer supporters looking for plain-language orientation and source-backed references;
  • researchers, students, writers, and journalists looking for a careful starting point;
  • organizations or policy audiences trying to understand why emotional awareness, communication, and support access matter.

People arrive with different needs. Some are looking for vocabulary, some for research, some for support options, and some for ways to explain alexithymia without shame or overstatement.

What “network” means here

“Network” means a public orientation and contact point around AAN’s mission. It does not mean that AAN verifies members, endorses providers, manages a clinical directory, or confirms that any listed person or organization offers alexithymia-specific services.

How to connect

If you want to suggest a correction, share a source, ask about collaboration, or point us toward a resource that should be reviewed, email team@alexithymiaawarenessnetwork.org.

Please do not send immediate safety, medical, or emergency requests to AAN. AAN is not equipped for that role. The Support page lists non-urgent practical support orientation.
