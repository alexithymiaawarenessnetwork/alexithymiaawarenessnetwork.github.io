Emotion researcher profile

James D. A. Parker

A concise, source-backed research-context note on James D. A. Parker's role in AAN's alexithymia assessment source trail.

This is a brief research-context page, not a full biography. It includes only claims AAN can support from citations and an official Trent University profile.

Why Parker appears on AAN

James D. A. Parker is included here because his name appears on several recurring AAN source trails for alexithymia assessment and affect-regulation research:

  • the 1994 TAS-20 item-selection and cross-validation paper, co-authored with R. Michael Bagby and Graeme J. Taylor (Bagby, Parker, and Taylor, 1994);
  • the 1997 book Disorders of Affect Regulation: Alexithymia in Medical and Psychiatric Illness, co-authored with Graeme J. Taylor and R. Michael Bagby (Taylor, Bagby, and Parker, 1997);
  • the 2006 Toronto Structured Interview for Alexithymia paper, co-authored with Bagby, Taylor, and Susan E. Dickens (Bagby, Taylor, Parker, and Dickens, 2006);
  • a 2020 review marking twenty-five years of the TAS-20 literature, co-authored with Bagby and Taylor (Bagby, Parker, and Taylor, 2020).

For AAN readers, the reason to include Parker is bibliographic and historical: his published work is part of the literature around the Toronto Alexithymia Scale-20 (TAS-20), the Toronto Structured Interview for Alexithymia (TSIA), and affect-regulation framing in alexithymia research.

Public profile evidence

Trent University’s Psychology department profile lists James Parker as a Professor and describes research interests in personality and affect regulation, including relationships among alexithymia, emotional intelligence, outcomes such as academic achievement and health problems, and psychometric tools for assessing alexithymia and emotional intelligence (Trent University profile, accessed 2026-07-02).

TAS-20 context

The TAS-20 is a self-report measurement instrument used in alexithymia research. AAN describes it as a screening or research measure, not as a diagnosis by itself. Scores should be interpreted carefully, especially outside research settings or without professional context.

What this page does not claim

This page does not publish a full career history, list grants or editorial roles, summarize emotional-intelligence research broadly, or claim that Parker’s work establishes any particular treatment for alexithymia.

This page also does not claim that alexithymia is a diagnosis or that any specific clinical decision follows from a TAS-20 score. AAN is informational and defers personal assessment or treatment questions to qualified clinicians.
