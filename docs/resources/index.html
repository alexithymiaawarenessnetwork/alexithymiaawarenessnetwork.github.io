Resource Categories

📚 Books

Books and handbook chapters can offer background on alexithymia, emotion research, and related topics. AAN does not treat books as diagnosis, treatment, or personal-care guidance.

🧭 Words for Feelings

A small vocabulary scaffold for comparing feeling words, body cues, intensity, and uncertainty. It is a reflection aid, not a diagnosis or settled theory of emotion.

📱 Mobile Apps & Digital Tools

A cautious guide to app and digital-tool claims, including why AAN is not recommending a specific app for alexithymia.

🔧 Assessment Instruments

A cautious overview of common alexithymia measurement tools, framed as measures or screens rather than diagnoses or treatment guidance.

🧭 Emotion Frameworks

A cautious map of basic-emotion, constructionist, core-affect, appraisal, affective-neuroscience, and vocabulary frameworks. It emphasizes where models are useful and where their assumptions conflict.

🗓️ Conferences & Events

How AAN handles event listings relevant to alexithymia, emotion research, education, and support.

📰 Research Papers

Sortable, searchable table of academic papers on alexithymia and emotional awareness.

🧪 Research Participation Studies

How AAN handles research participation listings related to alexithymia, emotional awareness, and adjacent topics.

🤝 Support

Urgent support information, therapist directories, online community links, and practical support pointers.

Getting Started

New to alexithymia?

  1. Read the FAQ for the most common questions
  2. Try the Words for Feelings lexicon as a small vocabulary scaffold
  3. Take a free online screening (it’s a screen, not a diagnosis)
  4. Join community discussion at r/Alexithymia

Looking for professional help?

  1. Read the FAQ sections on diagnosis boundaries and assessment instruments
  2. Read Diagnosis & Assessment and Assessment Instruments for non-diagnostic measurement boundaries
  3. Read the Treatment and Therapy Approaches pages for cautious, clinician-deferring treatment orientation
  4. Find therapist-directory starting points via Support
  5. Bring questions about assessment instruments, co-occurring conditions, and treatment goals to a qualified clinician

Interested in research?

  1. Search Research Papers (sortable / filterable)
  2. Check Research Participation Studies for participation-listing boundaries
  3. Read Emotion Frameworks for a caveated map of major emotion theories and vocabulary tools
  4. Treat profile pages as brief source-trail context, not full biographies or endorsement pages.

Contributing to Resources

Suggestions are welcome. We accept book and media-resource recommendations, verified tool links, links to organizations and services, and corrections. Email team@alexithymiaawarenessnetwork.org with details and we’ll review them for inclusion.

All resources are provided for informational purposes. For personalized advice and treatment, consult with qualified healthcare professionals.
