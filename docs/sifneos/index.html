Emotion researcher profile

Peter E. Sifneos

A brief, source-backed note on Peter E. Sifneos and the early alexithymia literature.

Peter E. Sifneos is included here because his 1973 paper, “The prevalence of ‘alexithymic’ characteristics in psychosomatic patients,” is the source AAN cites for the term entering the research literature (Sifneos, 1973).

This is a short historical note, not a full biography.

Why this paper matters

Sifneos’s 1973 paper gave researchers and clinicians a name for a pattern that later alexithymia literature continued to study: difficulty identifying and describing feelings, often discussed alongside an externally oriented thinking style (Luminet, Nielson, and Ridout, 2021). Modern AAN pages treat alexithymia as a dimensional construct, not as a diagnosis or a fixed identity category.

The original context was psychosomatic medicine. That history is important, but it should not be read as saying that alexithymia causes physical illness, that bodily symptoms are “just emotional,” or that one therapy approach is automatically appropriate for everyone.

What AAN can say

Source-backed public statements are narrow:

  • Sifneos authored the 1973 paper AAN cites as foundational to the alexithymia term.
  • The paper appeared in Psychotherapy and Psychosomatics in 1973 (citation record).
  • Later alexithymia research built out assessment tools, construct debates, and clinical questions beyond the original paper.
