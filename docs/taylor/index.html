Emotion researcher profile

Graeme J. Taylor

A concise, source-backed research-context note on Graeme J. Taylor's role in AAN's alexithymia assessment and affect-regulation source trail.

This is a brief research-context page, not a full biography. It includes only claims AAN can support from citations.

Why Taylor appears on AAN

Graeme J. Taylor is important to AAN’s alexithymia source trail because his name appears on two recurring reference points:

  • the 1997 book Disorders of Affect Regulation: Alexithymia in Medical and Psychiatric Illness, co-authored with R. Michael Bagby and James D. A. Parker (Taylor, Bagby, and Parker, 1997);
  • the 1994 TAS-20 development paper, co-authored with R. Michael Bagby and James D. A. Parker (Bagby, Parker, and Taylor, 1994).

For AAN readers, the reason to cite Taylor is historical and bibliographic: his work is part of the published literature around alexithymia assessment, especially the Toronto Alexithymia Scale-20 (TAS-20), and the affect-regulation framing used in alexithymia research.

TAS-20 context

The TAS-20 is a self-report measurement instrument used in alexithymia research. AAN describes it as a screening or research measure, not as a diagnosis by itself. Scores should be interpreted carefully, especially outside research settings or without professional context.

What this page does not claim

This page does not verify a current institutional affiliation, list grants or leadership roles, or summarize a full career history.

This page also does not claim that alexithymia is a diagnosis or that any specific treatment follows from a TAS-20 score. AAN is informational and defers personal assessment or treatment questions to qualified clinicians.
