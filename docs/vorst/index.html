Emotion researcher profile

Harrie C. M. Vorst

A source-cautious profile of Harrie C. M. Vorst's connection to the Bermond–Vorst Alexithymia Questionnaire.

Harrie C. M. Vorst is listed, with Bob Bermond, as an author of the 2001 validation paper for the Bermond–Vorst Alexithymia Questionnaire (BVAQ), a self-report alexithymia measure used in research (Vorst and Bermond, 2001).

This page is deliberately brief and does not present a full biography.

BVAQ relevance

The BVAQ is an assessment instrument, not a diagnosis. AAN describes it as one research measure among several alexithymia instruments. The BVAQ is especially relevant to AAN because its validation paper is part of the citation trail for assessment pages and FAQ wording about alexithymia measurement.

In public-facing AAN content, BVAQ claims should stay modest:

  • It is a self-report questionnaire used in alexithymia research.
  • It is associated with cognitive and affective dimensions in the BVAQ literature.
  • It should not be used by itself to diagnose a person or replace professional assessment.
  • Detailed scoring, cutoffs, translations, and clinical-use claims should come from qualified sources in an appropriate setting.

Name note

AAN uses “Harrie C. M. Vorst” because the BVAQ validation paper lists that form of the name. Other name variants should not be treated as public biographical claims without their own sources.
