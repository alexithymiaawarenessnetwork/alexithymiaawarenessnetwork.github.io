Bridging the gap between emotions and understanding.

The Alexithymia Awareness Network (AAN) is dedicated to increasing awareness, supporting research, and providing resources for individuals affected by alexithymia — a personality trait that affects how people identify, understand, and express emotions.

It affects roughly 10% of the general population and can shape relationships, mental health, and quality of life.

What is alexithymia?

What is Alexithymia?

Alexithymia, from the Greek meaning “no words for emotions,” is associated with difficulty identifying emotions, difficulty describing emotions, limited emotional awareness, and externally oriented thinking.

Core features

Difficulty identifying emotions, difficulty describing emotions, limited emotional awareness, and an externally oriented style of thinking form the basic explanation.

What it is not

Alexithymia is not listed as a disorder in the DSM-5 or ICD-11. The FAQ frames it as a dimensional trait that can co-occur with a range of medical and psychiatric conditions.

Assessment

There is no formal clinical diagnosis of alexithymia. The site points visitors toward validated assessment instruments such as TAS-20, BVAQ, PAQ, TSIA, and OAS, while clarifying that screening tools are not diagnoses.

A note for people with alexithymia

Physical distress counts too.

People with alexithymia may notice the physical signs of severe distress — chest tightness, nausea, sleep loss, agitation, dissociation — before the emotional signs become clear. If your body is sounding alarms and you cannot name what you are feeling, that is still a good enough reason to reach out.
